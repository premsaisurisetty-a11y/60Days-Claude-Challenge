
# Day 7 – Personalized Claude Usage Strategy

## Objective

Learn how to use Claude more effectively by creating a personalized AI workflow based on my goals, learning style, daily activities, and career aspirations.

---

# What I Worked On

Today I used the Claude Usage Strategy prompt to generate a personalized framework for how I should use Claude AI in different situations.

The strategy was customized based on:

* Current Situation: Student
* Activities: Coding, Development, Business Planning, Marketing
* Usage Frequency: Daily
* Output Needs:

  * Deep Research
  * Coding Assistance
  * Creative Content

The goal was to identify:

* Which Claude model to use
* Which effort setting to use
* Best practices
* Common mistakes to avoid
* A repeatable workflow for maximum productivity

# Biggest Mistakes To Avoid

### 1. Using Opus For Everything

Not every task needs maximum intelligence.

Sonnet is sufficient for most daily tasks.

---

### 2. Using Max Effort For Simple Tasks

Max effort should be reserved for complex work.

---

### 3. Writing Vague Prompts

Better context leads to better outputs.

---

### 4. Not Iterating

Follow-up prompts improve results significantly.

---

### 5. Skipping Context

Always provide:

* Role
* Goal
* Audience
* Constraints

# Key Learnings

### 1. Different Models Serve Different Purposes

Each Claude model has strengths depending on the task.

### 2. Effort Settings Matter

Using the correct effort level improves efficiency.

### 3. Sonnet Is The Best Daily Driver

For students and developers, Sonnet offers the best balance of speed and quality.

### 4. Context Improves Results

Providing detailed context produces more useful outputs.

### 5. AI Works Best Inside A System

A repeatable workflow creates consistency and better outcomes.

---

# Biggest Insight

The most effective AI users are not the people who ask the most questions.

They are the people who build repeatable systems around AI.

# Reflection

Today's challenge helped me understand how to use Claude strategically rather than randomly. By matching the right model and effort setting to each task, I can save time, improve output quality, and become more productive in learning, development, content creation, and career growth.
